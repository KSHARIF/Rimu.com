Favourite Poem:

I’m from dull september evenings, A light drizzle against a hospital window. From a tidy room and its sole occupant And the man who waited outside in the gloom. I’m from endless skies and not enough crayons From hand-me-downs and bougainvillea, The patter of feet on a gravelly path And humming that filled the air like perfume. I’m from cardboard boxes and the smell of ink, Duct tape and decades old furniture. From home to home to home And home a little more than a signature I’m from the dust between pages And thumbprints on spines; From pale limbs and sunshine That streamed in on lazy afternoons. I’m from words that scarred And a few that healed. From words treasured in velveteen journals And forgotten on newspaper margins. I’m from reckless laughter and quiet sighs - A solitary soul in a crowded room - From anguish and tears and bitter white lies And the thirst tearing at the back of my throat. I’m from darkness that seeped into the light Indigo nights, lavender mornings And cottony clouds, that caressed my wounds And the strange aftertaste of epiphanies. I’m from what I made of myself And some of what they made of me.

Love for Flowers

As I believe that Flowers are a gift to this earth. As Gerard De Nerval once said, “Every flower is a soul blossoming in nature.” They paint contrasting colors along the ground and bring joy to many. Their elegance and delicate touches have us all in awe and exhilaration. This is often why flowers are seen as loving gifts for the special people in our lives, and the reaction of the person receiving the flowers is priceless.

Love for Gardening

My garden is my most beautiful masterpiece. Gardening is how I relax. It’s another form of creating and playing with colors. I grow plants for many reasons: to please my eye or to please my soul, to challenge the elements or to challenge my patience, for novelty or for nostalgia, but mostly for the joy in seeing them grow. I like gardening. It’s a place where I find myself when I need to lose myself.
